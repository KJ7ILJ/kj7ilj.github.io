    Railgun Final Project
    For the 2019-20 school year, Emmett Hart and I are building a small railgun. Due to quarantine/school being canceled we are having schedule issues, as we do not have access to any of the tools or materials at school. So far we have gotten some small but noticeable force out of the railgun (lengthening a projectile's coasting range on a track beyond that which could be achieved with the injector only) but we do not have the equipment to make it go any faster since our power supply is a jump starter that I found in the garage and not a big capacitor bank.


    EZ-I65 Datalogging Rocket
    This rocket was my final project for my Intermediate Engineering class during the 2018-19 school year. It carried several sensors mounted on a Raspberry Pi hat that I designed in Autodesk EAGLE. The launch of this rocket got me a junior level 1 HPR certification. My partner for the project was Emmett Hart. The rocket was named "Jean 1" in honor of a nuke dropped on Thailand in our school's Model UN club.

    Miniature, Sub-Zero ATMEGA328P-AU Microcontroller
    A condensed microcontroller based on the ATMEGA328P-AU designed to work in sub-zero temperatures. Primary applications include areas where other microcontrollers could not function due to low temperatures or near-space ballooning.


    Level Shifting Micro SD Breakout Board
    This was the second PCB that I made. It has a voltage regulator, 8 channel level shifter, and a micro SD slot. Unfortunately, I misinterpreted the datasheet and ended up with the SD slot facing backwards... Nonetheless all the connections were still in the right place. I'm only putting it here to document its creation.


    PCB Yagi Tool
    A tool I wrote for fun in C++. It calculates trace dimensions for PCB-embedded Yagi antennas. A video of the tool in operation can be found here.


    CC1125RHBT Breakout Board
    A breakout board (my first 4-layer PCB) that matches the example schematic in the Texas Instruments CC1125RHBT's datasheet. It's basically a small digital radio that operates (in this setup) in the 400MHz range. It has 50 ohm traces and a connector for an SMA antenna.
