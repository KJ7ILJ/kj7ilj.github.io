    • 03/03/2020 - Railgun Recalculation

    As you can see from February 27th's post, our railgun's capacitor bank in series configuration does not provide sufficient current. So, here's the math for a parallel capacitor bank. Note that with a sufficient number of capacitors hooked up in series, the high voltage will outweight the compounding ESR and you'll end up with a higher maximum current. However, with only 3 or 4 capacitors, parallel is the better way to hook up the capacitor bank. Let's calculate the velocity of the projectile once more, but this time with the capacitors in parallel.

    Equations: $$F=IL\times B$$ $$I=\frac{V}{R}$$ $$R=\frac{\rho L}{A}$$ $$I=\frac{0.5V_R}{\frac{\Delta t}{C}+ESR_{DC}}$$ $$B=\frac{\mu_0}{2\pi}\frac{I}{r}$$ $$a=\frac{F}{m}$$ $$v_f^2=v_0^2+2a\Delta x$$
    Necessary Values: $$L=0.1m$$ $$V_R=2.7V$$ $$\Delta t=1s$$ $$ESR_{DC}=0.0029\Omega$$ $$\mu_0=4\pi *10^{-7}$$ $$m=0.005kg$$ $$r=0.01m$$
    So, now for the calculations. I'm not going to explain everything here, since I already did in the above-mentioned post from February 27th. $$\rho_{copper}=1.68*10^{-8}\Omega *m$$ $$A=3.95*10^{-5}m^2$$ (3/8" OD tube, 1/4" ID, 1/4 tube used) $$l=0.1m$$ $$R=\frac{1.68*10^{-8}\Omega *m *0.1m}{3.95*10^{-5}m^2}=4.25*10^{-4}\Omega = 0.000425\Omega$$ There are 2 rails in series, so the total resistance is: $$2*0.000425=0.00085$$ And now we can calculate the current: $$I=\frac{0.5V_R}{\frac{\Delta t}{C}+ESR_{DC}}$$ $$I=\frac{0.5*2.7V}{\frac{1s}{1080F}+(0.00085+(3/0.0029)^{-1})}=492.23A$$ That's significantly better, but still not super great. It's workable though. Let's calculate the velocity of a 5 gram projectile. $$B=\frac{\mu_0}{2\pi}\frac{I}{r}$$ $$B=\frac{4\pi *10^{-7}}{2\pi}\frac{492.23}{0.01m}=0.01969T=196.9G$$ $$F=IL\times B$$ $$F=(492.23A)(0.1m)\times 0.01969T=0.96N$$ $$a=\frac{0.96N}{0.005kg}=192\frac{m}{s^2}$$ $$v_f^2=v_0^2+2a\Delta x$$ $$v_f=\sqrt{2a\Delta x}=6.19m/s$$ Better, but still not great. We'll do some tests, perfect our equation, then build a bigger capacitor bank.
